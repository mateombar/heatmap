Working with scales

With the same data each chart is telling us a different story
  • Those who earn up to $45.200 dollars a year are lower class.
  • Those who earn between $45.200 and $135.600 dollars a year are middle class.
  • Those who earn more than $135.600 dollars a year are upper class.
  • Maximum income: $300.577

Linear Scale

It works by mapping the range of the input domain to a range of colors in the output range.

Quantize Scale

There are more individuals earning at most $100.192.

It works by dividing the range of the input domain by the number of buckets in the output range.

  • min $0 - max $100.192
  • min $100.192 - max $200.385
  • min $200.385 - max $300.577

Quantile Scale

There is an even amount of individuals in each class.

It works by dividing the number of items of the input domain by the number of buckets in the output range.

  • Lowest: 33
  • Middle: 33
  • Highest: 34

Threshold Scale

This scale tells us that there is a massive disparity between social classes.

There are more individuals in the lower class than the middle and upper classes combined.

It works by dividing the number of items of the input domain by the thresholds in the output range.

  • min $0 - max $45.200
  • min $45.200 - max $135.600
  • min $135.600 - max $300.577
